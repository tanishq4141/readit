# Deep Learning Systems Engineering

A practical, systems-oriented handbook for engineers transitioning into AI systems.

## The Goal
This is not a textbook. It is a field guide. It is designed to take a strong engineer and teach them the mechanical sympathy required to build, debug, and scale deep learning systems in production. We do not stop at "how to use PyTorch." We explore *why* architectures evolved, *what* breaks when you scale them, and *how* to fix the inevitable gradient failures.

## Target Audience
Engineers who learn by building. If you want architecture reasoning over memorization, debugging war stories over clean toy datasets, and systems thinking over academic proofs, this book is for you.

## Core Philosophy
Every chapter follows a strict mental model to bridge theory and production:
- **SPARK:** The intuition, the failure mode, the uncomfortable truth.
- **FORGE:** The math, the from-scratch implementation, the dissection of the naive approach.
- **WIRE:** Production deployment, scaling bottlenecks, debugging, and real-world engineering constraints.
